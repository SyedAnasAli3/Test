Comet Neowise
Star-gazing

After chasing NEOWISE for 3 nights, I'm super ecstatic and humbled to capture this beauty with my entry-level gear, zipping past Earth at a mind boggling speed of 230,000 km/h. Thanks to less light pollution, you can see the 2 split tails. The blue tail is due to ion discharge and the other tail is from dust. There was a third red-colored tail which can't be seen anymore as the comet is moving away from the Sun. Soon the tail will get fainter as it keeps moving away from our Sun.
